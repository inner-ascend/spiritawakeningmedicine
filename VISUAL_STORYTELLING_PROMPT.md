### Phase 2: Content Transformation - Less Sales, More Story

#### 2.1 General Principles:
‚úÖ **DO:**
- Write in first person (we/I) when authentic
- Share personal experiences and observations
- Use specific, sensory details
- Show the journey, not just the destination
- Acknowledge complexity and difficulty
- Honor the medicine and lineage

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Use marketing language ("transform your life", "breakthrough", "unlock")
- Make promises or guarantees
- Pressure people to apply
- Oversell the experience
- Remove safety warnings or contraindications

#### 2.2 Sections to Rewrite (Examples):

**BEFORE (Sales-y):**
> "Our ceremonies offer a profound opportunity to transform your life and breakthrough limiting patterns."

**AFTER (Storytelling):**
> "In the quiet of the Mazunte jungle, something shifts. Not always what you expect. Not always comfortable. But often, deeply true."

---

**BEFORE (Sales-y):**
> "Apply now to secure your spot in our next retreat!"

**AFTER (Storytelling):**
> "If this feels like your path, reach out. We'll talk. Sometimes it's a yes, sometimes it's not yet, sometimes it's a gentle no."

---

**BEFORE (Promotional):**
> "I'm a certified facilitator with extensive training in..."

**AFTER (Human):**
> "I didn't come to this work by accident. My own journey with plant medicine cracked me open, showed me edges I didn't know I had. Now I sit with others in that same sacred terror and beauty."
